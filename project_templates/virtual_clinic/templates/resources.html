Medical Camera

Back to Top

The camera can be used to send real-time video and images, either as an otoscope (for ear, nose, and throat images) or a dermascope (for skin images). To use the camera as an otoscope, the black otoscope base should be used, and the disposable otoscope tip attached to the top of the base. A new otoscope tip should be used for each patient. There are two sizes of tips, large and small, which come in clear packaging. The larger tip has a wider diameter head than the smaller tip. To use the camera as a dermascope, the clear dermascope base should be used. There is a cap on the base, which should be removed to take images and replaced once completed. When attaching the dermascope base, slowly twist the base around until the groove fits smoothly into the groove of the camera. Do not force the clear dermascope base onto the camera head.

The camera can zoom in and out on the subject by rolling the grey, ridged dial on the side of the camera. The light brightness can be controlled with the thin, black dial at the bottom of the device (near the wire). The bases are attached at the camera head. The wire on the camera ends with a USB connector. This connector should be plugged into the laptop when ready to use. The system will automatically pick up that the camera has been plugged in, and allow the user to switch cameras to this camera view. While the medical camera is turned on, both the webcam view and the medcial camera will be streamed to the remote side, and this is demonstrated on the platform.

How To Use the Medical Camera

Materials:

  • Medical Camera
  • Dermascope Base
  • Otoscope Base
  • Disposable Otoscope Tip
  • USB Extension Cable (optional)
  1. Plug the USB connector of the medical camera into the USB port of the computer. If using a USB cable extension, plug in the medical camera USB to the extension cord, and then the USB connection into the computer USB port.
  2. Confirm that the medical camera icon (4th icon from the right) is now enabled.
  3. Click the medical camera icon to turn on the medical camera on the consultation platform. You should now see the camera display on screen.
  4. Clean the base(s) that will be used with an alcohol wipe and allow to dry.
  5. If you are capturing skin images, place the dermascope base on the camera head. You may need to twist the base around the camera head until the grooves align. Do not force the base into the camera head. Then, remove the cap of the dermascope base.
  6. If you are capturing ear, nose, or throat images, place the otoscope base on the camera head. Place a new disposable tip on the otoscope base by twisting clockwise and locking it into place.
  7. Adjust the light source of the camera as necessary by using the thin black dial near the bottom of the device.
  8. Adjust the focus of the camera as necessary by turning the large silver dial on the side of the camera.
  9. Use the camera to examine the patient. Follow the 'How-To Take a Screenshot' guide to learn how to capture images from the medical camera and save them to the patient record.
  10. When the exam with the camera is finished, turn off the medical camera using the medical camera icon. Unplug the medical camera from the USB port.
  11. Remove and dispose of the otoscope tip, if one was used.
  12. Clean the dermascope or otoscope base with an alcohol wipe.
  13. Store away the camera and bases safely.

Stethoscope

Back to Top

The digital stethoscope can be used to send real-time audio from the patient. It can be used as a typical stethoscope, by placing the stethoscope diaphragm on the chest and abdomen to hear body sounds. The eartube can be used for the local user who is with the patient, to hear the sounds. The aux/USB connection can be plugged into the computer to send the sounds to the remote user (doctor). The eartube port on the stethoscope body has a headphones icon and is on the top of the device. The connection port is slightly on the side of the device, and has a wire icon.

The stethoscope can be turned off and on with the power button on the side. When on, the light on the front and back of the stethoscope body will glow white. When off, the light will not glow. The volume can be controlled by the + and - buttons on the side of the device. The device is battery powered and has a micro-USB port for charging. When the device battery is low, the lights on the body will glow red when the device is turned on. When charging, a red light indicates charging, and green light indicates a full charge. It is recommended to charge the device at least once a month (even when the device is not in use) to maintain the battery level, otherwise there is a risk of depleting the battery and needing replacement.

For the connection port, some computers will not recognize the device using the aux connection only. Therefore, an aux→USB connection has been provided. This is a small black connection which has an aux port at the bottom and a USB at the other end. This USB plugs into the computer so that the computer will recognize the device. Once plugged in, the application will recognize the stethoscope device and automatically allow the user to switch to this audio. When the stethoscope audio is playing, the other side will not hear the computer microphone (and will not hear the user speaking).

How To Use the Stethoscope

Materials:

  • Digital Stethoscope
  • Eartube
  • Aux to USB Adapter
  • Alcohol Wipes
  • USB Extension Cable
  1. Plug the USB connector of the USB to Aux adapter into the USB port of the computer. If using a USB cable extension, plug in the adapter USB to the extension cord, and then the extension cord USB connection into the computer USB port.
  2. Confirm that the stethoscope icon (3rd icon from the right) is now enabled.
  3. If not already plugged in, plug the aux cable into the aux port on the side of the stethoscope.
  4. If not already plugged in, plug in the eartube to the headphone port on the top of the stethoscope.
  5. Power on the stethoscope with the power button on the side. Confirm that the white light in the centre of the device is lit up.
  6. Clean the stethoscope diaphragm and the eartube with the alcohol pad.
  7. Indicate to the online user that you will be using the stethoscope now, noting that once you turn on the stethoscope, they will no longer be able to hear you, only the stethoscope audio. You will, however, still be able to hear the online user speaking.
  8. Turn on the stethoscope audio by pressing the stethoscope icon on the consult platform.
  9. Put on the eartube and use the stethoscope diaphragm to examine the patient.
  10. Adjust the volume as necessary by using the volume buttons on the side of the stethoscope.
  11. When the examination is completed, turn of the stethoscope audio using the stethoscope icon on the consultation platform.
  12. Unplug the stethoscope from the computer.
  13. Power off the stethoscope. Confirm that the white light on the stethoscope is off.
  14. Clean both the eartube and stethoscope diaphragm with an alcohol pad.
  15. Store away the stethoscope, eartube, and connections. The stethoscope may be stored with the eartube and aux/USB connector remaining plugged into the stethoscope ports.

Vital Signs Monitor

Back to Top

The vital signs monitor measures pulse oxygen level (SpO2), heart rate (HR), blood pressure, and temperature. It also measures ECG and respiration rate, which are discussed in the ECG section. The ports are color-coded (green and blue) based on the connection, with green being the ECG and temperature probe and blue being the SpO2 and HR probe. To connect the probes, gently slide the connector into the port, and twist to align the grooves. Do not force the connector into the port. The connector will make a click when it is fully connected. To remove the connector, gently pull on the grey connector until the connector slides out. For the blood pressure cuff, there is a white connector which should enter the middle port. Ensure that the metal part with the up arrow is fully pushed in before attempting to connect the blood pressure cuff. The blood pressure connector will click when it is fully connected. To release the blood pressure cuff, press the metal part with the up arrow and remove the connector.

The pulse oximeter should be placed on the finger with the finger icon side on the back of the hand (not the palm). The finger should be placed fully into the pulse oximeter, with the fingertip touching the opposite side. The blood pressure reading should not be taken on the same side at the same time as the pulse oximeter, or results may be incorrect.

The vital signs monitor can be turned on by pressing the grey selector button in the middle of the device. Once turned on, the selector button can be used to navigate through the selections on the bottom of the screen using short presses of the button. From left to right, the selections are: alarms on/off, Bluetooth on/off, start blood pressure test, power on/off. The currently selected icon will be larger than the other icons. To select the icon, long press the selector button for 2 seconds. This will change the state of the on/off buttons, or in the case of the blood pressure test, will trigger the start of the blood pressure test. If there is no blood pressure cuff plugged in, the test will not trigger. Starting the test will inflate the cuff and take the blood pressure reading. The test will be approximately 10 seconds, and when completed will output a reading next to the SYS and DIA headings on the interface.

When the pulse oximeter is plugged in, and the patient has the monitor on, a heart rate plot will begin to appear in the top third of the screen with a red line. After approximately 10-15 seconds, the pulse oximeter and heart rate values will then be displayed (next to SpO2 and HR labels, respectively). These will continuously change as they are monitoring the patient in real time. Similarly, the temperature monitor can be connected and the probe (small black circular piece on the ECG wire) placed in the armpit of the patient. The temperature will take approximately 60 seconds to get the temperature of the patient. Once the temperature stops changing, or is centring around a common value, this can be used as the patient's temperature.

All of these measurements are recorded in the patient's record by manually typing the values into the consultation platform. The values will automatically be sent to the remote user in real-time during the consultation.

How To Use the Vital Signs Monitor

Materials:

  • Vital signs monitor
  • SpO2 cable
  • Blood pressure cuff
  • ECG/Temperature cable
  • Alcohol pads
  1. Plug in the pulse oximeter, blood pressure cuff, and/or ECG/temperature cable (depending on which measurements are desired). When plugging in the blue/green cables, twist the cable base around the port until the grooves align and then push in. Do not force the cable ends into the port. For the blood pressure cuff, ensure that the metal piece with the arrow has been pushed in toward the port in order to open the port for the blood pressure cuff to be plugged in.
  2. Clean the monitors to be used on the patient with an alcohol wipe (finger monitor, cuff, temperature probe).
  3. Turn on the vital signs monitor using the grey button in the centre of the device.
  4. If collecting heart rate or SpO2, place the patient's finger inside the monitor, ensuring the fingertip touches the edge of the opposite end. Ensure that the fingertip icon is on the backside of the hand, not the palm side.
  5. The heart rate should now appear on screen as a waveform. Allow approximately 10 seconds for the HR and SpO2 measurements to appear on screen.
  6. If collecting blood pressure, place the cuff on the patient's arm. Ensure the patient is sitting, relaxed, and their legs are uncrossed. Do not take the HR and SpO2 measurements at the same time as the blood pressure.
  7. To start the blood pressure exam, press the middle grey button on the vital signs monitor until the blood pressure icon (3rd icon from the left) is highlighted. Long press on the grey button to start the test.
  8. Wait for the test to complete. The systolic and diastolic measurements will appear onscreen once the test is complete. If the measurements do not appear, it means the test has failed - ensure that the cuff is plugged in properly and placed correctly on the patient.
  9. If collecting the patient temperature, place the temperature probe in the armpit of the patient and have the patient close their armpit over the wire. Monitor the temperature reading on the device until it maintains a steady value (approximately 1 minute).
  10. Record any of the necessary measurements in the notes panel on the consultation platform.
  11. To record and send ECG data, review the 'How To Collect ECG Data' guide.
  12. When the examination is completed, remove the monitor/cuff/probe from the patient. To remove the cables, gently pull the cable base from the port. For the cuff, press the metal tab with the arrow to release the cable. Clean off the materials with an alcohol wipe.
  13. Navigate to the power option on the selection menu using the grey selector button. When the power button is highlighted, long press to select, turning off the device.
  14. Store the device and cables securely.

ECG

Back to Top

The ECG measurements are taken from the vital signs monitor, but because they cannot be manually typed in, there are further steps that need to be taken to send the data to the remote user. The patient should be attached to the ECG leads using the disposable electrodes. The electrodes should be clipped to the ECG leads before placing the electrodes on the patient.

There are 3 leads of the ECG: white right arm (RA), black left arm (LA), and red left foot (LL). They are placed to form Einthoven's triangle. The RA and LA electrodes should be placed under the clavicle, on the 2nd intercostal space, in line with one another but on their respective sides. The LL electrode should be placed on the lower left abdomen (does not need to be placed on the foot, despite its name).

Once the electrodes are placed, the vital signs monitor should be turned on (if not already on). If the electrodes have been placed properly, the respiration rate (RR) measurement will appear. The Bluetooth module must be used to read the ECG data. The virtual clinic has a local program which should be run on the computer to get the results. This will already be running in the background. The ECG panel can then be opened on the consult platform. 'Start ECG' will start the ECG test. To prevent failure of the test, ensure that the Bluetooth module on the device is blinking in the top right corner near the battery symbol. If you do not see the Bluetooth symbol, select it on the selector menu with the selector button. The test will return an ECG waveform to the consultation platform which is automatically shared with the remote user. The ECG test can be repeated multiple times. Note that the ECG waveform will not appear on the vital signs monitor in the top third of the screen; this waveform will be the heart rate only. There is no way to display the ECG waveform directly on the PM6100 device.

How To Take ECG Readings

Materials:

  • Vital signs monitor
  • ECG/Temperature cable
  • 3 disposable electrodes
  • Conduction gel (optional)
  1. Plug in the ECG/Temperature cable to the green port on the vital signs monitor.
  2. Turn on the vital signs monitor using the grey selector button.
  3. Clip the three electrodes to the red, white, and black leads of the ECG cables and ensure they are securely attached.
  4. (Optional) place a small amount of the conductive gel on the electrodes to improve measurements. This may not be necessary for certain electrodes with built-in conductive gel.
  5. Remove the sticker backing of the three electrodes and place them on the patient. White - RA, placed on the right chest. Black - LA, placed on the left chest. Red - LL, placed on the left side of the lower abdomen (refer to reference material for detailed placement instructions).
  6. Check that the heart rate waveform is appearing on the vital signs monitor. The respiration rate measurement should also appear.
  7. If it is not already open, go to the Virtual Clinic program on the PC home screen and double-click the icon to open it. It should open a small black prompt with some text. Do not exit or take any further action with this program.
  8. Go to the web application and click on the ECG icon to open the ECG panel at the bottom of the screen.
  9. Press 'Start ECG' on the consultation platform ECG panel.
  10. Use the grey selector button on the vital signs monitor to navigate to the Bluetooth icon (second icon from the left). Long press the button. Check the top corner of the screen; if there is not a blinking Bluetooth symbol, then long press the grey button again until it appears.
  11. The ECG data should appear after approximately 10-15 seconds as a graph on the consultation platform. The online user will also see this data automatically. If there is an error reading the data, ensure again that the Bluetooth icon is blinking in the top right corner and start the test again. The test can be taken as many times as necessary.
  12. If you wish to record the ECG data to the patient record, follow the 'How To Screenshot' guide.
  13. When the examination is completed, close the ECG panel using the ECG icon on the consultation platform.
  14. Remove the ECG leads from the patient. Dispose of the ECG electrodes.
  15. Unplug the ECG cable from the vital signs monitor by gently pulling on the bottom of the cable. Store the vital signs monitor and cable away securely.

Blood Test

Back to Top

The Accu-Answer blood test kit contains 4 different blood tests: uric acid, total cholesterol, haemoglobin, and blood glucose. The test devices are included in the black zipper pouch, and the test strips are in the blue Accu-Answer box in their respective containers. Each test strip container displays a two-digit code on the back, which allows for calibration of the device. The device will already be calibrated, but if there are consistent errors with using the device it may need to be recalibrated using these figures. Instructions on how to calibrate are provided in the pack.

The test device contains a port for the test strips and an eject button on the top to remove test strips once the testing is completed. There are also disposable lancets to be used in the lancing device to draw blood from the patient. The lancing device has a twisting head which allows for control of the depth of the needle, a plunger to load the lancet once it is placed inside, and the lancing button to deploy the lancet (pierce the patient). Once the lancet has been used, the head of the lancing device can be removed by pulling up and twisting. The lancet ejector can be slid up to remove the lancet safely. Only one lancet per patient should be used, and the lancets should be disposed of properly. The lancing device should be cleaned between each patient.

Some of the tests require a larger volume of blood. Ensure that enough blood is drawn from the patient before placing the blood on the test strip, otherwise an error will result. If the test fails, remove the test strip, draw more blood from the patient, and place a new test strip to try the test again. All results should be recorded manually in the consultation notes.

Make sure to keep the lid of the test strip container closed when not in use. Exposure to light can reduce the effectiveness of the test strips.

How To Conduct a Blood Test

Materials:

  • Blood test zipper pouch, which includes:
    • Lancing device
    • Disposable lancets
    • Blood test device
  • Blood test strip containers
  • Alcohol pads
  • Plaster or cotton for puncture site (optional)
  1. Clean your hands and the patient's hands.
  2. Prepare the lancing device. Remove the lancet head by twisting anti-clockwise and pulling from the lancet body.
  3. Place a new lancet into the lancet body. Twist off the cap of the lancet to remove it and expose the lancet needle.
  4. Replace the lancet head by twisting it clockwise back onto the lancet body.
  5. Clean the lancet head using an alcohol pad.
  6. Prepare the test strip and testing device. Place the desired testing strip into the test strip port of the left side of the testing device.
  7. Collect the blood from the patient by first cleaning the piercing site with an alcohol pad. Adjust the lancing device depth appropriately. Pull back the plunger of the lancing device.
  8. Place the lancing device head against the piercing site finger. When ready, press the button on the side of the lancing device to deploy the lancet and pierce the finger. Remove the device and squeeze out a large drop of blood on the patient's finger.
  9. Place the test strip against the blood drop to soak the blood on the testing strip. A countdown should start displaying on the testing device. Note that a large drop of blood is needed for the test. If an error displays on the device instead of a countdown, the likely reason for error is because the blood drop was not enough. Repeat the test with a larger amount of blood.
  10. Clean off the patient finger with an alcohol pad and place the plaster or cotton on the puncture site as necessary.
  11. Clean the lancing device head with an alcohol pad.
  12. Repeat as necessary for the desired tests. Note that a drop of blood will be needed for each test. Record the measurements as taken in the notes section of the consultation platform.
  13. When the examination is completed, carefully remove the lancing device head by twisting anti-clockwise and pulling the head off. Remove the lancet by sliding the grey remover up on the lancing device and dispose of the needle properly. Replace the head of the lancing device.
  14. Remove the test strip from the device by pressing the eject button on the top of the testing device. Dispose of the test strip properly.
  15. Replace all materials back in the zipper pouch and stow away the zipper pouch and test strips securely.

Patient Records

Back to Top

The patient record page can be accessed from the homepage menu under 'view patient records.' The desired patient can be searched and the book icon can be clicked to go to their record.

The patient record page contains a header with the basic demographic patient information, a photo, and patient profile options. Below this are three sections, from left to right: patient charts, consultation data, and patient information. Each of these sections can be minimised to reduce the information on the screen. The charts section contains charts of the measurements recorded for the past 3 visits for SpO2, heart rate, blood pressure, blood glucose, and weight. This can be customised to look at a 6 or 9 visit timespan if desired. The consultations section contains all data from all previous consults, including notes, relevant images, dates, and times. It is displayed in reverse chronological order. Each consultation can be edited to add information after the consultation is completed by pressing the edit button; however, note that information is recorded including a trail of who edited which consultations, and what information was changed. Finally, the patient information includes the medical history as well as contact information for the patient. This can also be edited using the 'edit patient' button in the top right corner. To start a consultation with the patient, the 'start consult' button can be pressed. The 'print/export patient data' button is used to print patient data from the chart or to export it to another digital system.

Add a Patient

Back to Top

If the patient is not listed in the system, they must be added to the system with the 'add patient' option (found on the homepage, top menu, or on the patient list page). Each section must be expanded to see the form. The personal information section includes required fields, but all other information is optional (though helpful to include). The 'Save' button within each section or the 'Save Patient' button at the bottom of the page will save the patient to the database, given all required fields are completed. Name, ID, and date of birth cannot be changed once the patient is created. All other fields can be changed over time. Once the patient is created, they are listed on the patient record list and their patient record is created. They may now have consultations using the system.

Consultation Platform

Back to Top

The consultation platform is where consultations between patient/nurse and doctor are conducted. It includes a real-time video connection, and connects with the medical devices to share patient information. There are several features included in the consult platform. The header menu has 9 options for nurse users and 7 options for doctor users (the medical camera and stethoscope options are not available for doctor users as they will not access these devices). The shared menu features include a translation panel for translating language (spoken and written), a notes panel for recording consultation data, a recording button to begin recording the consult session, on/off toggles for microphone and webcam inputs, an ECG panel allowing the user to record and transmit ECG from the vital signs monitor, and a helpdesk panel which allows the user to look up resources from the helpdesk while in the consultation.

How To Translate

Back to Top
  1. Open the translation panel by selecting the translation panel icon (first on the left) on the menu in the consultation platform.
  2. If recording speech, select the language to record speech from.
  3. Press 'start recording speech' and have the speaker begin talking. When completed, press ‘stop recording speech.' Note that it is better for performance to do small chunks of speech (less than 20 seconds) rather than longer speech.
  4. The results will appear in the editable box below the recording button. Have the speaker quickly review the text (if possible) to ensure it's accuracy. Edit the text by typing if there are any mistakes.
  5. If the speaker does not wish to speak to translate, they may also type in the speech input box directly.
  6. Select the language to translate the text into.
  7. Press the 'translate' button to translate the text from the input box. The result will appear in the second (lower) input box.
  8. Each of the text results will automatically be sent to the other user. To access this as the other user, open the translation panel and monitor the input boxes.
  9. Translate back and forth as necessary. To adjust the models used for translation and transcription, select 'advanced settings' at the bottom and customise as desired.

How To Take a Screenshot

Back to Top
  1. Arrange the camera view as desired on the consultation platform.
  2. When you are ready, select the screenshot icon in the top left corner of the video or ECG chart that you would like to take a picture of.
  3. The picture is now recorded to the patient's record and is available to view after the consultation has ended.

How To Record a Session

Back to Top
  1. Select the recording icon (3rd from the left) on the top menu in the consultation platform.
  2. A popup will appear asking you to select a screen to record. Go to the 'Screen' section at the top. You will see a picture which should look like what you see on your screen while in the consultation. Click this box.
  3. In the bottom left corner, there is a checkbox which says 'share audio.' Make sure this checkbox is selected.
  4. Select the blue 'share' button at the bottom.
  5. The recording will now be started.
  6. When you end the consultation, the recording will be processed and saved to the patient's record and available to review.

How To Save and End the Consultation

Back to Top
  1. 1. Data from the consultation automatically saves to the patient's record as it is entered. You do not need to press 'save' at any point during the consultation. The patient data is also automatically sent to the other user on the consultation - there is no further action you need to take to do this.
  2. 2. When the consultation is completed, either user can select 'end consultation' which will end the consultation for both users. A loading screen will then appear. Do not click out of the loading screen, as the data is being processed. Once the processing is completed, you will be redirected to the patient record, where you can review and confirm the details from the consultation just conducted.
